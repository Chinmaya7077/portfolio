BLOG: Why I Use ClickHouse for Trade Analytics

PostgreSQL is great for OLTP, but when you need to aggregate billions of rows in under 2 seconds, ClickHouse is the answer.

THE PROBLEM:
PostgreSQL handled our OLTP workload perfectly - orders, wallets, user state. But analytical queries over trade history took 45 seconds on 10M rows. Unacceptable for a real-time dashboard.

ENTER CLICKHOUSE:
ClickHouse is a columnar OLAP database. The same query runs in 0.8 seconds on the same data.

Why it's faster:
- Columnar storage: only reads the columns you need
- Vectorized execution: processes data in CPU-cache-friendly batches
- Compression: 10x smaller on disk than PostgreSQL for the same data

ARCHITECTURE:
I use PostgreSQL for writes (orders, wallets) and replicate trade events to ClickHouse via Kafka:
FastAPI -> PostgreSQL (orders) -> Kafka -> ClickHouse (analytics)

This gives ACID for writes and blazing-fast analytics for reads. Best of both worlds.
